## ## Git Is Important

Version control is one of the foundational tools of modern software development. Whether you're building an application by yourself or collaborating with hundreds of engineers, Git provides a shared history of how a project evolves over time.

That doesn't mean your workflow should be complicated.

Establish the simplest Git workflow that meets your team's needs. Simpler workflows are easier to understand, easier to teach, and often easier to debug when something goes wrong.

As your team grows or your product becomes more complex, your workflow can evolve. Until then, avoid introducing branching strategies, release processes, or conventions that solve problems you don't actually have.

Git is already powerful enough. Your goal is to make collaboration predictable, not elaborate.

Like many engineering decisions, the best workflow is often the one that introduces the least complexity while still solving the problem in front of you.
