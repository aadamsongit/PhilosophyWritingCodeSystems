## Open Source Etiquette

Open source has its own culture and expectations. Maintainers volunteer significant time reviewing contributions, answering questions, and caring for projects over long periods. Respecting that effort is an important part of being a good contributor.

In recent years, many maintainers have expressed concerns about low-quality AI-generated pull requests and contribution campaigns that encourage quantity over quality. While these efforts are often well intentioned, they can create additional review work without providing meaningful value to the project.

Before opening a pull request, take time to understand the project's contribution guidelines, coding conventions, and goals. Read existing issues, communicate with maintainers when appropriate, and aim to contribute something that genuinely improves the project. Open source is ultimately about collaboration, not simply increasing your contribution count.
