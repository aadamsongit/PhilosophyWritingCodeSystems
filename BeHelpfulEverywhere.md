# Be Helpful Everywhere

One of the best ways to build a professional reputation is to be genuinely helpful.

Answer questions in Discord communities. Participate in Reddit discussions. Contribute on Stack Overflow. Help a classmate. Mentor someone with less experience. Learn from someone with more experience.

Not every interaction will lead to a job, nor should that be the goal. Helping people develops technical communication exposes you to unfamiliar problems, and reinforces your own understanding. Teaching often reveals gaps in your own mental models that solving problems alone does not. It also creates goodwill. People tend to remember engineers who explain difficult concepts clearly, contribute thoughtfully, and make technical communities more welcoming. Be careful not to overextend yourself. Your own learning and work still deserve time and attention. But when you have the opportunity to help someone sincerely, take it. Software engineering is a collaborative profession. 

The habits that make you a valuable teammate often begin long before you join a team. Mentor and be mentored. Teach and be taught. Ask good questions. Give thoughtful answers. Be helpful wherever you are. Do you know what product spaces interest you? Can you help others reason through choices, participate in discussion, 
or contribute to documentation? 

## Unblock Workflows

One of the highest-leverage engineering skills is learning how to unblock yourself and others. Sometimes the best solution is writing code. Other times it is improving a workflow, clarifying requirements, fixing tooling, writing documentation, or automating a repetitive task.

Optimize intentionally. Not every bottleneck is worth solving, and premature optimization can create unnecessary complexity. Learn to identify the real constraint, determine whether it is worth addressing, and choose the simplest solution that meaningfully improves the workflow.

Removing friction often creates more value than adding another feature.
