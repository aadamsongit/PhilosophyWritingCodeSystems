## No- or Low-Code Prototyping Can Validate Behavioral UX

Not every product idea needs to begin with production code. No-code and low-code tools can be valuable for validating user behavior, navigation, and workflow before investing significant engineering effort.

Simple prototypes, wireframes, clickable mockups, and forms can reveal whether users understand an interface, complete a workflow, or interact with a feature as intended. These prototypes are not replacements for well-engineered software, but they can answer important product questions early and reduce the cost of changing direction.

Prototype at the level of uncertainty. If you're unsure about the user experience, prototype the experience. If you're unsure about the architecture, prototype the architecture. If you're unsure about the implementation, build a small vertical slice.
