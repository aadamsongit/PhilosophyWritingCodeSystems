## Prefer

* Gather requirements from stakeholders.
* Translate those requirements into a technical plan.
* Refine the plan as new information emerges.
* Develop enough business understanding to recognize why a feature exists, who it serves, and what success looks like.
* Build durable mental models that transfer across languages, frameworks, and products.

## Do Not Prefer

* Collecting frameworks, libraries, and isolated facts without understanding when or why to use them.
* Treating technologies like collectibles rather than tools for solving problems.
* Optimizing for trivia instead of engineering judgment.

The goal isn't to know the most technologies. It's to understand problems well enough to choose appropriate solutions.

## Golden Rules

1. **Do not approve pull requests you don't understand.**

   Whether the code was written by a teammate, an AI assistant, or your past self, your responsibility is the same. A pull request should follow the team's conventions, solve the intended problem, and be understandable to the people who will maintain it.

2. **Reason before you generate.**

   AI can accelerate implementation, but it cannot replace your understanding of the system. Build a mental model first. Then evaluate every suggestion against that model.

3. **Optimize for durable understanding.**

   Frameworks, tools, and interview trends will change. The ability to gather requirements, decompose problems, communicate clearly, and exercise engineering judgment will continue to transfer across technologies.

4. **Be the Gandalf in code review.**

   Your job is not to slow progress. Your job is to protect the quality of the codebase. Ask questions. Challenge assumptions. Demand explanations. Vibe-coded pull requests shall not pass without understanding.
